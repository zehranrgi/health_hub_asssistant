COMMON MEDICATIONS - CVS HEALTH INFORMATION

=== LISINOPRIL (Blood Pressure Medication) ===
Category: ACE Inhibitor
Uses: Treats high blood pressure (hypertension) and heart failure. Helps prevent strokes, heart attacks, and kidney problems.
Dosage: Typically 10-40mg once daily. Take at the same time each day.
Side Effects: Dizziness, headache, persistent dry cough, fatigue. Serious: swelling of face/lips/tongue, difficulty breathing.
Warnings: Do not use if pregnant. May cause dizziness - avoid driving until you know how it affects you.
Interactions: Potassium supplements, NSAIDs (ibuprofen), diuretics. Consult pharmacist.
Source: FDA approved medication information

=== METFORMIN (Diabetes Medication) ===
Category: Antidiabetic (Biguanide)
Uses: Controls high blood sugar in type 2 diabetes. Helps prevent kidney damage, blindness, nerve problems, and cardiovascular disease.
Dosage: Usually 500mg-2000mg daily with meals. Extended-release: once daily with evening meal.
Side Effects: Nausea, stomach upset, diarrhea, metallic taste. Usually improves over time.
Warnings: Risk of lactic acidosis (rare but serious). Avoid alcohol. Tell doctor before any surgery or X-ray procedures.
Interactions: Certain blood pressure medications, diuretics, corticosteroids may affect blood sugar control.
Monitoring: Regular blood sugar testing and HbA1c checks every 3 months.
Source: FDA approved medication information

=== ATORVASTATIN (Lipitor - Cholesterol Medication) ===
Category: Statin
Uses: Lowers bad cholesterol (LDL) and triglycerides, raises good cholesterol (HDL). Prevents heart attacks and strokes.
Dosage: 10-80mg once daily, can be taken any time of day with or without food.
Side Effects: Muscle pain/weakness, headache, nausea, joint pain. Rare: liver problems, memory loss.
Warnings: Avoid grapefruit juice (can increase medication levels). Report unexplained muscle pain immediately.
Interactions: Some antibiotics, antifungals, and other cholesterol medications.
Lab Tests: Liver function tests before starting and periodically during treatment.
Source: FDA approved medication information

=== AMLODIPINE (Norvasc - Blood Pressure Medication) ===
Category: Calcium channel blocker
Uses: Treats high blood pressure and chest pain (angina). Relaxes blood vessels.
Dosage: 2.5-10mg once daily. Can be taken with or without food.
Side Effects: Swelling in ankles/feet, dizziness, flushing, fatigue, palpitations.
Warnings: May cause dizziness especially when starting. Rise slowly from sitting/lying position.
Interactions: Grapefruit juice, certain antibiotics, antifungals, simvastatin.
Benefits: Works gradually - may take 1-2 weeks to see full blood pressure reduction.
Source: FDA approved medication information

=== OMEPRAZOLE (Prilosec - Heartburn/GERD Medication) ===
Category: Proton pump inhibitor (PPI)
Uses: Treats frequent heartburn, GERD, stomach ulcers. Reduces stomach acid production.
Dosage: 20-40mg once daily, 30-60 minutes before a meal. Short-term use recommended (4-8 weeks).
Side Effects: Headache, nausea, diarrhea, stomach pain, gas.
Warnings: Long-term use may increase risk of bone fractures, vitamin B12 deficiency, kidney problems.
Interactions: May affect absorption of certain medications including blood thinners, antifungals, HIV medications.
Important: Take before eating for best effect. Do not crush delayed-release capsules.
Source: FDA approved medication information

=== LEVOTHYROXINE (Synthroid - Thyroid Medication) ===
Category: Thyroid hormone replacement
Uses: Treats hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid). Replaces or supplements thyroid hormone.
Dosage: Individualized based on blood tests. Take on empty stomach, 30-60 minutes before breakfast.
Side Effects: Usually minimal when properly dosed. Over-medication: rapid heartbeat, anxiety, weight loss, tremors.
Warnings: Takes 4-6 weeks to see full effect. Do not switch brands without consulting doctor.
Interactions: Calcium, iron supplements, antacids reduce absorption - take 4 hours apart.
Monitoring: TSH blood tests every 6-12 months to adjust dose.
Source: FDA approved medication information

=== ASPIRIN (Pain Relief & Heart Protection) ===
Category: NSAID / Antiplatelet
Uses: Low-dose (81mg): Prevents heart attacks and strokes. Regular strength (325mg): Pain, fever, inflammation.
Dosage: Heart protection: 81mg daily. Pain: 325-650mg every 4-6 hours as needed.
Side Effects: Stomach upset, heartburn, increased bleeding risk, ringing in ears (high doses).
Warnings: Do not use in children with viral infections (Reye's syndrome risk). Increases bleeding risk.
Interactions: Blood thinners, other NSAIDs, alcohol increase bleeding risk.
Important: Take with food to reduce stomach upset. Consult doctor before daily aspirin therapy.
Source: FDA approved medication information
