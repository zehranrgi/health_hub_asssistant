=== MEDICARE COVERAGE ===

Medicare Part B (Medical Insurance):
Covers at CVS:
- Flu shots (100% covered, no copay)
- Pneumonia vaccines (100% covered)
- Hepatitis B vaccine (if high-risk, 100% covered)
- COVID-19 vaccines (100% covered)
- Certain diabetic supplies
- Some preventive services at MinuteClinic

Medicare Part D (Prescription Drug Coverage):
- Covers prescription medications
- Copays vary by plan and drug tier
- Generic drugs typically $0-$10 copay
- Brand drugs: higher copays ($30-$100+)
- Specialty drugs: often percentage-based copay (25-33%)

Coverage Phases:
1. Deductible phase: Pay full cost until deductible met
2. Initial coverage: Pay copays based on tiers
3. Coverage gap ("donut hole"): Pay 25% of costs
4. Catastrophic coverage: Pay small amount after out-of-pocket limit

Extra Help (Low-Income Subsidy):
- Reduces or eliminates Medicare Part D costs
- Automatic for those on Medicaid or SSI
- Application: ssa.gov or 1-800-772-1213

Medicare Advantage Plans:
- Many include prescription coverage
- May have different copays than original Medicare
- Check formulary for covered medications

CVS Medicare Services:
- Medicare Part D plan comparison assistance
- Medication therapy management (MTM)
- Compliance programs
- Pharmacist consultations

=== MEDICAID COVERAGE ===

Eligibility: Varies by state
Coverage at CVS:
- Prescription medications (formulary-based)
- Many preventive services
- Vaccines
- MinuteClinic services (most states)

Copays:
- Typically $0-$5 per prescription
- May waive copays for certain populations
- Preventive services usually $0 copay

State Programs:
- Each state manages own Medicaid formulary
- Prior authorization may be required for some medications
- Quantity limits may apply

How to Use:
- Present Medicaid card at pharmacy
- Pharmacist verifies eligibility
- May need prescriber information

=== COMMERCIAL INSURANCE ===

Typical Coverage Tiers:
Tier 1 (Generic): $5-$15 copay
Tier 2 (Preferred Brand): $30-$50 copay
Tier 3 (Non-Preferred Brand): $60-$100 copay
Tier 4 (Specialty): $100+ or 25-33% coinsurance

Deductibles:
- Medical deductible may apply to prescriptions
- High-deductible health plans (HDHP): pay full cost until deductible met
- Copays may not count toward deductible (varies by plan)

Out-of-Pocket Maximum:
- After reaching annual limit, insurance pays 100%
- Typical range: $2,000-$8,000 per person

Formulary:
- List of covered medications
- Preferred drugs have lower copays
- Non-formulary drugs: higher cost or not covered
- Formulary changes annually (sometimes mid-year)

Prior Authorization:
- Required for certain expensive or specialty medications
- Doctor must provide medical justification
- CVS pharmacists can help initiate PA process
- Usually takes 24-72 hours for approval

=== PRESCRIPTION COST-SAVING STRATEGIES ===

1. Generic Substitution:
- Generics are 70-80% cheaper than brand names
- FDA-approved as equivalent to brand
- Ask pharmacist about generic options
- Insurance usually requires generic if available

2. 90-Day Supply:
- Often 2-3 times cheaper than three 30-day fills
- Available for maintenance medications
- Ask doctor for 90-day prescription
- May require mail order or CVS pharmacy

3. Manufacturer Copay Cards:
- Reduce or eliminate copays for brand medications
- Available for most brand-name drugs
- Find at drug manufacturer website or ask pharmacist
- Cannot combine with government insurance (Medicare/Medicaid)

4. Patient Assistance Programs:
- Free or low-cost medications for uninsured/underinsured
- Income-based eligibility
- CVS pharmacists can provide information
- Resources: NeedyMeds.org, RxAssist.org

5. GoodRx and Discount Cards:
- Alternative pricing for uninsured or high copays
- Compare prices at CVS.com or GoodRx
- Cannot combine with insurance
- Sometimes cheaper than insurance copay

6. Therapeutic Substitution:
- Different medication in same class
- May be preferred on formulary (lower copay)
- Discuss with doctor and pharmacist

7. Pill Splitting:
- Higher dose split in half = lower cost
- Only for scored tablets (safe to split)
- Ask doctor to prescribe double strength
- Requires pill splitter device

8. CVS Prescription Savings Club:
- $15/year individual, $20/year family
- Discounts on select generics and brand drugs
- For uninsured or medications not covered by insurance
- Additional discounts on CVS Health products

=== SPECIALTY MEDICATION COVERAGE ===

Characteristics:
- Treat complex/rare conditions (cancer, MS, hepatitis C, etc.)
- Very expensive ($1,000-$10,000+ per month)
- May require special handling or administration
- Often covered under medical benefit instead of pharmacy benefit

Insurance Coverage:
- Usually Tier 4 or Tier 5
- Coinsurance instead of copay (25-33% of cost)
- May have separate deductible
- Annual out-of-pocket max still applies

Financial Assistance:
- Manufacturer patient assistance programs
- Copay assistance foundations
- CVS Specialty Pharmacy helps identify programs
- Financial counseling available

CVS Specialty Pharmacy:
- Coordinates insurance benefits
- Handles prior authorizations
- Connects to financial assistance
- Provides clinical support
- Delivers to home

=== PRIOR AUTHORIZATION PROCESS ===

When Required:
- Expensive medications
- Drugs with abuse potential
- Medications requiring step therapy
- Non-preferred formulary drugs
- Quantity limits exceeded

Steps:
1. Pharmacist attempts to fill prescription
2. Insurance requires prior authorization
3. Pharmacist contacts prescriber
4. Doctor submits medical justification to insurance
5. Insurance approves or denies (24-72 hours typical)
6. If denied, appeal process available

CVS Support:
- Pharmacists initiate PA process
- Follow up with insurance and doctor
- Notify patient of status
- Suggest covered alternatives if denied

=== VACCINE COVERAGE ===

Preventive Vaccines (Typically 100% Covered):
- Flu vaccine
- COVID-19 vaccine
- Pneumonia vaccine (age 65+)
- Shingles vaccine (covered by Medicare Part D, commercial insurance)
- Tdap/Td booster
- Hepatitis A & B (if high-risk)

Where Covered:
- Medicare Part B: Flu, pneumonia, COVID-19, Hepatitis B (high-risk)
- Medicare Part D: Shingles, other vaccines
- Commercial insurance: Most vaccines as preventive care (no copay)
- Affordable Care Act: All recommended vaccines covered

Travel Vaccines:
- Usually not covered by insurance
- Pay out-of-pocket
- May be reimbursed by HSA/FSA
- Prices available at MinuteClinic

=== HSA/FSA (TAX-ADVANTAGED ACCOUNTS) ===

Health Savings Account (HSA):
- Paired with high-deductible health plan
- Contributions tax-deductible
- Use for prescriptions, copays, medical expenses
- Funds roll over year to year
- CVS accepts HSA debit cards

Flexible Spending Account (FSA):
- Use-it-or-lose-it each year (some plans allow $500+ rollover)
- Pre-tax contributions
- Use for prescriptions, OTC medications, health products
- CVS accepts FSA debit cards

Eligible Expenses at CVS:
- Prescription medications
- Insulin (no prescription needed as of 2020)
- Many OTC medications (with prescription)
- First aid supplies
- Diabetic supplies
- Blood pressure monitors
- COVID-19 tests
- Sunscreen (SPF 15+)
- Contact lens solution

Check Eligibility: FSAstore.com for complete list

Options for Uninsured:

Cash Pricing:

=== CLAIMS AND REJECTIONS ===

Common Rejection Reasons:
- Not covered by insurance (not on formulary)
- Prior authorization required
- Quantity limits exceeded
- Refill too soon
- Plan limitations or exclusions
- Incorrect insurance information

What CVS Does:
- Pharmacist investigates rejection
- Contacts insurance for clarification
- Suggests alternatives if available
- Helps with prior authorization
- Provides cash pricing options

Appeals Process:
- If medication denied, can appeal to insurance
- Doctor provides medical necessity documentation
- CVS can assist with appeal process
- External review available if internal appeal denied
