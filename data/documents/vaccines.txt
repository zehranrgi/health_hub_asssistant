CVS PHARMACY - VACCINE INFORMATION

=== INFLUENZA (FLU) VACCINE ===
Category: Annual Vaccine
Availability: September through March (peak flu season)
Who Needs It: Everyone 6 months and older, especially high-risk groups (65+, chronic conditions, pregnant women)
Types Available:
- Standard flu shot (ages 6 months+)
- High-dose flu shot (ages 65+, stronger immune response)
- Flu shot for egg allergy patients
Schedule: Once per year, preferably by end of October
Side Effects: Soreness at injection site, mild fever, body aches for 1-2 days
Cost: Usually covered 100% by insurance, Medicare Part B
Walk-ins: Accepted at most CVS locations
Important: Takes 2 weeks to develop full immunity
Source: CDC Vaccine Guidelines

=== COVID-19 VACCINE ===
Category: Updated Annual Vaccine
Availability: Year-round, updated formulas each fall
Who Needs It: Everyone 6 months and older
Brands Available:
- Pfizer-BioNTech (mRNA)
- Moderna (mRNA)
- Novavax (protein-based)
Schedule:
- Primary series: 1-2 doses depending on age and vaccine type
- Boosters: Recommended annually, especially for 65+ and immunocompromised
Side Effects: Arm soreness, fatigue, headache, mild fever, chills (usually 1-2 days)
Cost: Covered by insurance and Medicare. Free for uninsured through federal program.
Important: Can be given same time as flu vaccine
Appointments: Recommended, but walk-ins accepted
Source: CDC and FDA Emergency Use Authorization

=== SHINGLES VACCINE (Shingrix) ===
Category: Two-dose series
Availability: Year-round
Who Needs It: Adults 50 years and older, even if had shingles before
Why Important: Prevents shingles (painful rash) and post-herpetic neuralgia (long-lasting nerve pain)
Schedule: Two doses, 2-6 months apart. Both doses required for full protection.
Effectiveness: Over 90% effective at preventing shingles
Side Effects: Sore arm, fatigue, muscle aches, headache, fever (stronger reaction than most vaccines, lasts 2-3 days)
Cost: Covered by Medicare Part D and most insurance plans
Important: Much more effective than older shingles vaccine (Zostavax)
Note: Side effects are normal and indicate immune response is working
Source: CDC ACIP Recommendations

=== PNEUMOCOCCAL VACCINE (Pneumonia) ===
Category: Pneumonia prevention
Availability: Year-round
Who Needs It:
- All adults 65 and older
- Adults 19-64 with certain conditions (diabetes, heart disease, lung disease, smoking)
- Children under 5
Types:
- PCV20 (Prevnar 20): One dose for most adults
- PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23): May need in addition to PCV20 for some patients
Schedule: Usually one-time vaccination, sometimes requires 2 doses 1 year apart
Side Effects: Mild soreness, redness at injection site, mild fever
Cost: Covered by Medicare Part B and most insurance
Important: Prevents serious pneumonia, meningitis, bloodstream infections
Source: CDC Pneumococcal Vaccination Guidelines

=== TDAP VACCINE (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis) ===
Category: Booster vaccine
Availability: Year-round
Who Needs It:
- All adults once, then Td booster every 10 years
- Pregnant women during each pregnancy (27-36 weeks)
- Anyone caring for infants
Why Important: Prevents tetanus (lockjaw), diphtheria, whooping cough
Side Effects: Arm soreness, redness, mild fever, headache
Cost: Covered by insurance and Medicare
Important: Especially crucial for grandparents and caregivers of newborns
Emergency: Need Td/Tdap within 48 hours of dirty wound if not current
Source: CDC Vaccination Schedule

=== HEPATITIS B VACCINE ===
Category: Three-dose series
Availability: Year-round
Who Needs It:
- Healthcare workers
- Dialysis patients
- People with diabetes under 60
- International travelers
- Adults at risk for hepatitis B
Schedule: 3 doses over 6 months (0, 1 month, 6 months)
Side Effects: Sore arm, mild fever, fatigue
Cost: Covered by most insurance; Medicare Part B for high-risk
Important: Provides long-term protection against liver disease and cancer
Source: CDC Hepatitis B Vaccination Guidelines

=== TRAVEL VACCINES ===
Category: Destination-specific
Available at CVS MinuteClinic locations
Common Travel Vaccines:
- Hepatitis A (food/water-borne illness)
- Typhoid
- Yellow Fever (specific clinics only)
- Japanese Encephalitis
- Rabies (for high-risk travel)
Planning: Schedule consultation 4-6 weeks before travel
Cost: May not be covered by insurance; prices vary
Important: Some countries require proof of vaccination (Yellow Fever)
Service: CVS MinuteClinic provides travel health consultations
Source: CDC Travelers' Health

=== HOW TO SCHEDULE VACCINES AT CVS ===
Methods:
1. Online: CVS.com/immunizations/covid-19-vaccine
2. CVS Pharmacy app
3. Call local pharmacy: Find at CVS.com/store-locator
4. Walk-in: Most locations accept walk-ins (wait times may vary)

What to Bring:
- Insurance card
- Photo ID
- Vaccination record card (if you have one)

Insurance:
- Most vaccines covered 100% by insurance as preventive care
- Medicare Part B covers flu, pneumonia, Hepatitis B (if high-risk)
- Medicare Part D covers shingles and other vaccines

Important Notes:
- Pharmacists can vaccinate patients 5 years and older (varies by state)
- MinuteClinic vaccinates all ages including infants
- Vaccine records uploaded to state immunization registry
- Can receive multiple vaccines in same visit (different arms)
- After vaccination: Stay 15 minutes for observation

Source: CVS Health Immunization Services
